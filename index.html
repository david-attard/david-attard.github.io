About

I am a third-year PhD student in Astrophysics at the University of Sussex, supervised by Prof. Ilian T. Iliev and supported by DISCnet. My research focuses on the Epoch of Reionization, early galaxy formation, and the role of radiative feedback in shaping dwarf galaxies.

I work with large cosmological simulation suites including HESTIA and MEGATRON, and I contribute to pyc2ray, a Python-based GPU-accelerated radiative-transfer code with performance-critical components written in Fortran.

Research

Local Group Reionization

I study how reionization progressed in a realistic Local Group environment using constrained HESTIA simulations and radiative-transfer post-processing. This work explores how the Milky Way and M31 progenitors were ionized and how external radiation fields shaped their early environments.

Dwarf Galaxy Quenching in the Epoch of Reionization

My current work, in collaboration with Martin Rey, uses the MEGATRON high-redshift simulation suite to investigate how low-mass dwarf galaxies become temporarily or permanently quenched at z ≃ 8.5. By comparing simulations with identical initial conditions but different feedback prescriptions, I test whether early quenching is driven primarily by sub-grid feedback physics or by rapid cosmological assembly.

Radiative Transfer and Lyman-Werner Feedback

I contribute to the development of pyc2ray, with current work focused on Lyman-Werner radiation, molecular hydrogen dissociation, minihalo feedback, and Pop III source prescriptions.
